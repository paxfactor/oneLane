About

It all started with a dream: to drink a lot. Like most people, I determined that I didn’t like beer when I started my drinking career. I was too cool for it, and when I would drink beer, it would be whatever cheap stuff someone could buy for me. Usually Corona. (Editor’s note: Underage drinking is illegal and not recommended, etc.) I still fondly remember the beer that kick-started my tastebuds: Left Hand’s Sawtooth Ale, at Microbrew Monday at Iguana Mack’s in Chandler, AZ. Something about that beer — refreshing, peppery, bitter, and most importantly, complex — tore through my “beer is gross” excuses.

Combine that with the explosion in craft breweries here in Phoenix, and the rise of DIY and homebrew communities, and you’ve got the foundations of a giant beer fan. I’ve found that beer is an art form, and can be just as subtle and brilliant as a good wine, and just as delicious as a gourmet dinner. And now if someone tells me they don’t like beer, I’ll just tell them they haven’t tried the right one. So crack open a cold one, check out my recipes, argue with your friends about space, and enjoy!

Recipes

All my recipes are extract or partial mash. Ingredients are for 5 gallon batches. These recipes are written assuming you already have some knowledge of the brewing process, but are otherwise fairly simple. If you plan on bottling, you can find a priming calculator here.

Swimsuit Saison

A super simple SMaSH saison. A great recipe to practice your technique.

OG: 1.063
IBU: 22
ABV: 6.2%

    Ingredients
  • Light Dried Malt Extract :: 7.0 lbs
  • Saaz hops :: 2.0 oz @ 60 min
  • Saison yeast

Mix malt extract into 3 gallons of water and bring to a boil. Stir constantly to avoid scorching until the extract is fully dissolved. Once boiling, add hops and boil for 60 minutes, stirring frequently. After boil completes, reduce wort temperature and add to fermenter with enough additional water to reach target gravity. Pitch yeast, aerate, and let ferment for at least one week.

Across the Blueniverse

A blueberry wheat beer, made in honor of my nephew Jude.

OG: 1.050
IBU: 12
ABV: 5.25%

    Ingredients
  • Wheat Dried Malt Extract :: 6.0 lbs
  • Munich (US) :: 1.0 lbs
  • Hallertauer hops :: 1.0 oz @ 60 min
  • Saaz hops :: 1.0 oz @ 15 min
  • American wheat yeast
  • Blueberries :: 3.0 lbs
  • Blueberry Extract :: 2.0 oz

Steep crushed grains in hot water (160 degrees) for 30 minutes. Rinse with cool water and mix in extract. Stir until extract dissolves, and bring to a boil. Add hops following the prescribed schedule and remove from heat after 60 minutes. Cool wort, add to fermenter, top with cool water to hit target gravity. Pitch yeast and ferment for one week.

After fermentation slows, transfer wort to a secondary fermenter. Soak the blueberries in vodka to disinfect, drain well, and add to secondary fermenter. Allow to ferment for an additional week. When bottling/kegging, add blueberry extract to taste (2 oz should be plenty).

Photo Finish

A Kentucky common, based on a pre-Prohibition style.

OG: 1.051
IBU: 13
ABV: 5.0%

    Ingredients
  • Light Liquid Malt Extract :: 6.6 lbs
  • Black Patent :: 0.25 lbs
  • Crystal 70-80L :: 0.25 lbs
  • Cluster hops :: 0.5 oz @ 60 min
  • Liberty hops :: 0.5 oz @ 15 min
  • Liberty hops :: 0.5 oz @ 0 min
  • American ale yeast

Steep crushed grains in hot water (160 degrees) for 30 minutes. Rinse with cool water and mix in extract. Stir until extract dissolves, and bring to a boil. Add hops following the prescribed schedule and remove from heat after 60 minutes. Cool wort, add to fermenter, top with cool water to hit target gravity. Pitch yeast and ferment for one week. This is designed to ferment and drink quickly, so there's no benefit to letting it age.

Bock in the Saddle

A Texas bock, my first attempt at lagering.

OG: 1.054
IBU: 19
ABV: 5.2%

    Ingredients
  • Light Dried Malt Extract :: 3.0 lbs
  • Dark Liquid Malt Extract :: 3.3 lbs
  • Crystal 120L :: 0.75 lbs
  • Cluster hops :: 1.0 oz @ 60 min
  • American lager yeast

Steep crushed grains in hot water (160 degrees) for 30 minutes. Rinse with cool water and mix in extract. Stir until extract dissolves, and bring to a boil. Add hops following the prescribed schedule and remove from heat after 60 minutes. Cool wort, add to fermenter, top with cool water to hit target gravity. Pitch yeast and ferment at lagering temperatures for at least two weeks. Don't forget the diacetyl rest! The longer you let this one rest, the better it gets.

CISSiPa

An IPA highlighting Galaxy hops. I made this one to celebrate passing the CISSP exam.

OG: 1.065
IBU: 67
ABV: 6.4%

    Ingredients
  • Light Dried Malt Extract :: 1.0 lbs
  • Light Liquid Malt Extract :: 6.6 lbs
  • Crystal 10L :: 1.0 lbs
  • Munich :: 0.5 lbs
  • Galaxy hops :: 1.0 oz @ 60 min
  • Galaxy hops :: 1.0 oz @ 30 min
  • Galaxy hops :: 1.0 oz @ 15 min
  • Galaxy hops :: 1.0 oz @ 0 min
  • Galaxy hops :: 1.0 oz @ dry hop
  • American ale yeast

Steep crushed grains in hot water (160 degrees) for 30 minutes. Rinse with cool water and mix in extract. Stir until extract dissolves, and bring to a boil. Add hops following the prescribed schedule and remove from heat after 60 minutes. Cool wort, add to fermenter, top with cool water to hit target gravity. Pitch yeast and ferment for 5-7 days. When fermentation starts to slow, transfer wort to secondary fermenter and add the dry hops. Let ferment for an additional week before bottling/kegging.
