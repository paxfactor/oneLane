About

It all started with a dream: to drink a lot. Like most people, I determined that I didn’t like beer when I started my drinking career. I was too cool for it, and when I would drink beer, it would be whatever cheap stuff someone could buy for me. Usually Corona. (Editor’s note: Underage drinking is illegal and not recommended, etc.) I still fondly remember the beer that kick-started my tastebuds: Left Hand’s Sawtooth Ale, at Microbrew Monday at Iguana Mack’s in Chandler, AZ. Something about that beer — refreshing, peppery, bitter, and most importantly, complex — tore through my “beer is gross” excuses.

Combine that with the explosion in craft breweries here in Phoenix, and the rise of DIY and homebrew communities, and you’ve got the foundations of a giant beer fan. I’ve found that beer is an art form, and can be just as subtle and brilliant as a good wine, and just as delicious as a gourmet dinner. And now if someone tells me they don’t like beer, I’ll just tell them they haven’t tried the right one. So crack open a cold one, check out my recipes, argue with your friends about space, and enjoy!
